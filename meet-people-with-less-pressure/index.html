Calmer introductions

A first message can feel simple again.

Chaput helps people meet through profiles and begin with one considered message. You have context before writing, the receiver has room to choose, and neither side needs to turn the opening into a performance.

Download ChaputLearn how it works

Start with what is already there

Look for a specific interest, project, photo detail or opinion the person chose to share. Ask a question that can be answered without giving away private information. The aim is not to impress everyone; it is to give one person a natural place to reply.

Make room for no

A follow request may be declined and a message may receive no answer. That is not an invitation to repeat the approach. Chaput’s one-message flow supports a clear pause, while blocking, restricting and reporting remain available when boundaries are ignored.

Keep expectations honest

Chaput can create a more considered starting point, but it cannot guarantee chemistry, friendship or a response. A good social experience still depends on patience, respect and the choices of both people.

Frequently asked questions

What should I write in a first message?

Mention a genuine profile detail and ask one specific, easy-to-answer question.

What if my follow request is declined?

Accept the decision and do not retry through another account or channel.

Does Chaput guarantee that someone will reply?

No. Chaput provides a structured way to begin, but every person decides whether to respond.
