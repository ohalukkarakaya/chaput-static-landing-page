Start with something real

The best opening messages are specific, easy to answer and connected to the profile. A question about a genuine interest gives the conversation somewhere to go.

Create from the pattern, not the person

Misunderstandings, timing, contrast and unexpected replies can all inspire original content. Use the underlying idea to create something new instead of copying a private person’s words or identity.

Meet alternative communities with respect

Shared music, art and culture can make conversation easier. Read the person behind the style, respect boundaries and avoid stereotypes, sexualisation or gatekeeping.

Frequently asked questions

Are these guides useful outside Chaput?

Yes. They explain Chaput where relevant, but each guide also offers practical ideas you can use elsewhere.

Do the guides encourage copying conversations?

No. They focus on learning from conversation patterns and creating original work with respect for privacy and consent.

Is Chaput a random-chat app?

No. Chaput uses profile-based discovery, and some connections require follow approval.
