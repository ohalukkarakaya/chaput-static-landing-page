Podcast planning guide

Find podcast topics people will want to keep discussing.

A strong episode begins with a question that matters to the listener, supports more than one credible point of view and has enough depth to reward attention. Build a question bank, then shape each idea into one clear episode promise.

Collect questions, not just trends

Listen for moments when people disagree about why something happened, what it costs or what should happen next. Listener messages, current events, books, research and community conversations can all reveal lasting questions.

Give the episode a clear promise

Write down what the listener will understand by the end, the central tension and the evidence you need. Choose guests with direct experience or relevant expertise, and leave space for the conversation to surprise you.

Use conversation as a starting point

Visible exchanges can reveal questions that make people pause, disagree or tell a story. Use that insight to plan original discussion, verify factual claims and protect private or identifying details.

Frequently asked questions

Where can I find podcast topics?

Start with listener questions, current events, books, research, specialist communities and recurring tensions in everyday conversation.

How do I know whether a topic can carry an episode?

It should make a clear promise, have meaningful stakes, support more than one credible view and offer enough material to explore.

Can Chaput inspire a podcast topic?

Conversation dynamics on visible profiles can suggest questions and themes, which you can develop through original research and reporting.
