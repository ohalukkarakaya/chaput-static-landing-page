Podcast planning guide

Find podcast topics with enough tension to carry an episode.

Good podcast topics begin with a question that has stakes, more than one reasonable viewpoint and enough evidence to explore. Build a question bank from listener problems, current events, research, communities and everyday conversations, then narrow each idea to one episode-sized promise.

Collect questions, not just trends

Trends can create timing, but a question creates an episode. Save moments where people disagree about cause, trade-offs or what should happen next. Listener messages, search questions, specialist newsletters, public records, books and community discussions can all reveal durable tensions.

Write each idea as a question a listener would care about. “Remote work” is a category; “what do teams lose when every difficult conversation moves to chat?” is a discussion with stakes.

Turn the question into an episode

Define the listener promise, the main tension and the evidence needed before recording. Gather at least two credible perspectives, mark what is fact versus opinion and decide what the episode will not try to solve.

For an interview, choose a guest with direct experience or defensible expertise. For a roundtable, give participants distinct roles instead of inviting several people to repeat the same view. A strong outline leaves room for surprise without losing the central question.

Use conversation sources responsibly

Visible conversations can show which questions make people pause, disagree or tell a story. Chaput may help creators observe those dynamics when privacy rules allow, but it is not a finished-script service or a licence to reproduce someone’s words.

Verify factual claims with primary or authoritative sources. If a real person could be identified, obtain appropriate permission or remove identifying details. Do not turn private pain, harassment or vulnerable disclosures into entertainment.

Frequently asked questions

Where can I find podcast discussion topics?

Start with listener questions, current events, research, specialist communities, books and recurring tensions in everyday conversation.

How do I know if a topic can carry an episode?

It should have a clear listener promise, meaningful stakes, more than one credible viewpoint and enough evidence for focused exploration.

Can Chaput provide podcast scripts?

No. Chaput is a social app that may inspire observations about conversation dynamics; it is not a script generator or licensed content library.
