Creator guide

Build a dialogue page people recognise in a single post.

Begin with a clear promise, two or three formats you can repeat without repeating yourself, and a voice that feels unmistakably yours. The strongest pages turn familiar conversation dynamics into original writing.

Decide what your audience should feel

Choose a focused territory: awkward replies, friendship dynamics, workplace tension, romantic misunderstandings or the relief of finally saying the right thing. A clear emotional promise makes every editorial decision easier.

Build formats that leave room for new ideas

Try a four-line misunderstanding, a “what would you reply?” prompt or a contrast between what someone says and means. Keep typography and pacing consistent while changing the situation and payoff.

Create an original idea pipeline

Collect themes from daily observations, audience questions, culture and conversations you are allowed to reference. Keep the tension or rhythm, then rewrite the people, setting, words and ending until the post is fully your own.

Frequently asked questions

What should a new dialogue page post first?

Start with two or three original formats built around one clear emotional or comedic promise.

Where can I find dialogue ideas?

Use daily observations, audience questions, public culture and conversation patterns, then transform them into original writing.

How often should the format change?

Keep enough consistency to be recognisable, but vary the setup, characters and payoff so the page stays fresh.
