For commentary creators

Find the bigger question inside a conversation.

A strong commentary topic often appears when an everyday exchange reveals something larger about boundaries, expectations, tone or the way people understand one another.

Download ChaputLearn how it works

Ask what the conversation is really about

Instead of repeating every line, identify the question underneath it. Was the problem timing, consent, a hidden expectation or a change in tone? That question gives your commentary a clear purpose.

Bring context to the reaction

Share what you can verify, separate fact from opinion and consider more than one reasonable interpretation. Remove identifying details when they add nothing to the discussion, and never use visibility as an excuse to direct harassment at someone.

Create a format people recognise

Recurring ideas such as “the moment the tone changed,” “what each person thought was happening” or “one better reply” give your work a consistent voice without depending on a single screenshot.

Frequently asked questions

Can Chaput inspire reaction videos?

Yes. Visible conversation dynamics can suggest topics, but the final analysis should be original and respectful of privacy and consent.

Should commentary identify the people involved?

Usually not. Focus on the idea unless identification is necessary, lawful and appropriately permitted.

Which conversation topics work well?

Boundaries, mixed signals, expectations, conflict repair and sudden changes in tone often lead to thoughtful commentary.
