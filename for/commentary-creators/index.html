For commentary creators

Turn a conversation pattern into a point of view.

Commentary starts when a conversation reveals a wider question: a boundary, misunderstanding, social rule or choice that people recognise in their own lives.

Download ChaputLearn how it works

Find the question beneath the exchange

Instead of retelling a conversation line by line, ask what it exposes. Was the problem timing, tone, consent, expectation or an unspoken norm? That question gives your commentary a reason to exist.

Add reporting, not just reaction

Explain the context you can verify, separate fact from opinion and show more than one plausible interpretation. Remove identifying details when they are not necessary, and never treat visibility as permission to target a person.

Build recurring formats

Try “the line where this changed,” “what each person thought was happening,” or “one better reply.” A consistent editorial lens is more durable than chasing isolated screenshots.

Frequently asked questions

Can Chaput inspire reaction videos?

Visible conversation dynamics can suggest topics, but creators should make original analysis and respect privacy and permissions.

Should commentary identify the people involved?

Usually not unless identification is necessary, lawful and appropriately permitted. Focus on the idea rather than directing attention at a private person.

What topics work well?

Boundaries, ambiguity, expectations, conflict repair and surprising changes in tone often create useful commentary questions.
