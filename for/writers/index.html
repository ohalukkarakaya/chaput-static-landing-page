For writers

Write online dialogue that feels typed, not staged.

Believable chat dialogue depends on what people omit, how quickly they answer and how each message changes the reader’s understanding of the one before it.

Write the gap between messages

Online dialogue has pauses, corrections, short replies and unfinished thoughts. Decide what happened during the silence and what each person is avoiding before polishing the words.

Give each message a job

A message can invite, deflect, test a boundary, repair tension or reveal that someone misunderstood. If several messages do the same job, combine or remove them.

Observe responsibly

Visible conversation patterns can help writers study rhythm without copying a real person’s identity or exact words. Build composite situations and transform the context until the scene belongs to your story.

Frequently asked questions

How do I make text-message dialogue realistic?

Use varied message lengths, purposeful pauses, subtext and reactions that change the direction of the exchange.

Can real conversations be writing prompts?

Patterns can inspire original scenes, but do not copy identifying details or private material without appropriate permission.

Why does chat dialogue feel different from spoken dialogue?

Timing, typing choices, missing body language and the ability to revise before sending all shape the scene.
