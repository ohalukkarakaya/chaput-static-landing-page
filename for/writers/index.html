For writers

Write online conversations that sound like real people.

Natural chat dialogue lives in the pauses, short replies and unfinished thoughts between the words. When every message changes the mood or reveals something new, the exchange begins to feel real.

Write what happens between messages

A delayed reply, a rewritten sentence or a sudden one-word answer can carry as much meaning as a long speech. Decide what each person is thinking during the silence before you refine the dialogue.

Give every message a purpose

A message can invite, avoid, reassure, challenge or reveal a misunderstanding. If several lines do the same job, combine them and let the conversation move.

Turn observation into original work

Pay attention to the rhythm of conversations around you, then change the characters, setting and outcome until the scene belongs entirely to your story. Protect private details and never reproduce someone else's personal exchange without permission.

Frequently asked questions

How can I make text-message dialogue feel realistic?

Vary the length of replies, use pauses with purpose and let each message change what the reader understands.

Can real conversations inspire fiction?

Yes. Observe their rhythm and tension, then transform the situation instead of copying personal details or exact wording.

Why does chat dialogue feel different from spoken dialogue?

People can pause, edit and choose not to answer, while tone and body language have to be carried by the words themselves.
