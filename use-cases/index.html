Use cases

A social app can begin with the conversation, not the feed.

Use Chaput to discover people through profiles, begin with one considered message and, where privacy rules allow, explore conversations that stay connected to their context.

Download ChaputLearn how it works

Meet people with less first-message pressure

A good first message does not need to perform. Read the profile, choose a real point of connection and leave one Chaput. The receiver can answer without being chased by a stream of follow-ups.

For a better opening
Mention a shared interest, ask a specific question and avoid assumptions about the person.

Follow conversation formats, not private lives

Visible conversations can reveal a funny reversal, an awkward pause, a strong opening line or a useful communication dilemma. Those patterns can inspire original commentary, voice-over, writing or meme formats.

Visibility is not a blanket licence to reuse someone else’s words or identity. Creators should respect privacy, permissions, platform rules and the people behind each conversation.

Find people around shared interests

Profile-first discovery can help people connect around music, art, games, local culture or alternative subcultures. Chaput is built to make the person and their context visible before the first message begins.

Frequently asked questions

Can creators use Chaput?

Creators can use visible conversation patterns as inspiration, while respecting privacy, permissions and the people involved. Chaput is not a stock dialogue library or licensing service.

Is Chaput only for creators?

No. Its main purpose is social discovery and conversation; creator inspiration is a secondary use.

Can I meet people by shared interests?

Yes. Profiles and suggested people can help you discover users whose interests give you a natural place to begin.
