The first Chaput

One message makes the first move. A reply makes the conversation.

On Chaput, a sender leaves one opening Chaput and waits. They cannot add repeated follow-ups until the receiver replies, giving the receiver a clear choice and giving the first message a reason to be thoughtful.

Why one message changes the tone

When there is only one opening, specificity matters. A reference to the profile or a simple, answerable question is more useful than a generic compliment. The rule also prevents the opening from becoming a stack of unanswered messages.

What happens next

If the receiver replies within the 24-hour response window, the Chaput opens into a conversation. If no reply arrives, it may move to the archive. Revive may be available for eligible archived conversations.

A limit, not a pressure tactic

The timer does not create an obligation to answer. Silence is a valid choice. Senders should not seek another route to repeat the message, and receivers can use reporting, blocking or restricting controls when needed.

Frequently asked questions

Can I send another message before a reply?

No. You can leave one opening Chaput and must wait for the receiver to answer.

Does the receiver have to reply within 24 hours?

No. The response window defines the product flow, not an obligation to respond.

What is Revive?

Revive can reopen eligible archived conversations. Availability depends on the conversation state and product rules.
