The first Chaput

One message opens the door. A reply starts the conversation.

Chaput keeps the beginning simple: read the profile, choose one genuine point of connection and send a single first message. The recipient can answer in their own time, without a pile of follow-ups waiting for them.

Download ChaputLearn how it works

Make the first message worth answering

Mention something specific from the profile and ask a question that feels easy to answer. A thoughtful sentence usually says more than several generic messages.

Let the reply be a real choice

After the first Chaput, give the other person room. If they reply, the conversation opens; if they do not, respect the silence and leave it there.

Keep your opening human

Write as you would speak to someone you genuinely want to know. Avoid copied lines, demands and assumptions about who they are.

Frequently asked questions

Why can I send only one first Chaput?

The single-message start gives the recipient space to choose whether they want to continue.

What should the first message say?

Refer to one real detail in the profile and ask a clear, considerate question.

What if I do not receive a reply?

Do not repeat the message. Respect the person's choice and continue discovering other profiles.
