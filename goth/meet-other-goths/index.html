Community guide

Let shared music and culture start the conversation.

Finding other goths becomes easier when you begin with the things that bring the culture to life: bands, club nights, art, books, photography, fashion and the projects people care about.

Download ChaputLearn how it works

Look for the detail that feels familiar

A band tee, an event, a handmade piece or a favourite book can create a genuine opening. Ask about what the person chose to share rather than judging whether they fit a label.

Be curious without testing anyone

No one should have to pass a music quiz or defend their style to deserve respect. Let people describe their own relationship with the culture in their own words.

Keep the first step thoughtful

Mention the shared detail and ask one clear question. Give the other person time to answer, and respect a declined request or silence without sending another message.

Frequently asked questions

Where can I meet other goths?

Music events, art communities, alternative-interest groups and profile-led social apps are useful places to begin.

What should I talk about first?

Choose a band, event, artwork, book or project that you genuinely noticed in the profile.

Do I need to fit one goth style?

No. Goth culture is diverse, and people take part in it in many different ways.
