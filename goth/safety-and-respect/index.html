Community safety guide

Respect makes every community stronger.

A shared scene can make it easier to connect, but it never removes the need for consent, boundaries and ordinary online safety. Treat people as individuals, keep private information private and use safety controls when something feels wrong.

Respect the boundary the first time

Do not repeat unwanted messages, pressure someone to move off-platform or ask for personal details they have not offered. A decline, a block or silence does not need further explanation.

Avoid fetishisation and gatekeeping

No style makes someone available for attention, and no person has to prove that they are “goth enough.” Ask about what they share without reducing them to an aesthetic or testing their identity.

Use the tools available to you

Report harmful content, block abusive users and restrict interactions when needed. Before meeting anyone in person, choose a public place, tell someone you trust and keep control of your travel plans.

Frequently asked questions

What should I do if someone ignores my boundaries?

Stop engaging, use blocking or reporting tools and seek local help if you feel at immediate risk.

Is it respectful to comment on someone's style?

A sincere, non-sexual compliment can be fine, but it should not become an assumption about personality, availability or consent.

How can I make an in-person meeting safer?

Meet in public, tell someone you trust, arrange your own transport and leave if you feel uncomfortable.
