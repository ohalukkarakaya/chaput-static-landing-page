Safety and respect

Goth people are people, not fantasies.

Interest in an aesthetic never overrides a person’s boundaries. Chaput does not welcome harassment, unwanted sexualisation, stereotype-based treatment or repeated contact after no response.

Download ChaputLearn how it works

Behaviour that crosses the line

Unwanted sexual comments, repeated sexualised messages, treating someone as a “goth fantasy,” pressuring for photos or personal contact, insults after rejection, stalking, threats and targeting younger users are unacceptable.

Sexual interaction involving minors is prohibited. Never share a private conversation without appropriate permission.

What respectful contact looks like

Read profiles, ask about genuine shared interests, avoid assumptions, accept rejection and discuss music, art, events or culture naturally. A person can enjoy goth style without owing anyone attention, education or access.

Use the controls available to you

Users can report inappropriate users, profiles, messages or content in the app and can block or restrict accounts. Safety concerns should be reported with enough context for review. For an immediate threat, contact local emergency or law-enforcement services.

Product boundaries that support user choice

Private profiles by default, follow approval where required and one first Chaput before a reply create explicit decision points. These measures support boundaries but do not replace personal caution or guarantee safety.

Frequently asked questions

What is goth fetishisation?

It is treating a person primarily as a sexualised aesthetic fantasy or stereotype rather than an individual with boundaries.

What should I do after no response?

Do not send repeated messages or try another account. Accept the lack of response and move on.

Can I report harassment on Chaput?

Yes. Chaput includes in-app reporting, blocking and restricting tools.
