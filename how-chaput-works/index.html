How Chaput turns a first message into a conversation.

Find a profile, connect when approval is required, leave one first Chaput and give the other person room to decide whether the conversation begins.

Download ChaputLearn how it works

1. Discover a profile

Explore suggested people or search for someone by name or username. Profiles are private by default, so a follow request may need to be accepted before the next step becomes available.

2. Leave one intentional Chaput

Choose a genuine detail from the profile and write one opening message. The single-message rule means the receiver is not met with a stack of follow-ups before they have chosen to respond.

3. A reply opens the conversation

If the receiver replies within the 24-hour window, the Chaput becomes a chat. Conversations can stay attached to the profile tree when the relevant visibility rules allow; Whisper remains visible only to eligible participants.

If there is no reply, the Chaput may enter the archive. Revive can be available for eligible archived conversations.

What stays in your control

Users can manage follow relationships, report content, block or restrict accounts and use the visibility options available in the app. The exact audience for a conversation depends on the applicable profile and conversation settings.

Frequently asked questions

How many first messages can I send?

You can leave one initial Chaput and must wait for a reply before the conversation continues.

What happens after 24 hours?

An unanswered Chaput may move to the archive. Eligible archived conversations may later support Revive.

What is Whisper?

Whisper is a message layer that only eligible conversation participants can see.
